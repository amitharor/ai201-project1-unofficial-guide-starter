Wireless professional advice here... not amateur / consumer. If I were updating my tech today for a purposeful reason, like need more coverage, unit died, etc, I would go with a WiFI 6 or 6E supporting device. WiFi 7 is not a final approved standard. All WiFi 7 gear is based on a draft that won't likely see final approval until end of 2024. Most major vendors like Cisco, Meraki, Juniper, Aruba, etc, are saying 2025 (mid to late) for their WiFi 7 products to hit.

UI jumped the gun a bit like many of the consumer brands. Consumer brands throw this stuff out there knowing the consumers will fall prey to the desire for the latest. Commercial vendors know most commercial accounts could give two hoots... they want stable and reliable. Not bragging rights around the neighborhood.

There is nothing wrong with WiFi 5 today. The channels stay the same with WiFi 6. WiFi 6 does provide some better throughput and handling in dense environments, but if your clients are not all WiFi 6 or your needs are not that demanding, it really does not gain you any benefit. Other than being future proofed against something going EOL tomorrow.

The only reason I would go with WiFi 7 is if I wanted to support 6GHz options. And then, I would skip the U7-Pro and go with the U6-Enterprise or In Wall version, both supporting WiFi 6E and thus 6GHz. None of the WiFi 7 benefits are going to help you without WiFi 7 client support and a better backbone network to support 2.5Gbps.

Only you can decide. Everyone will have an opinion. Some will swear it is their way or no other. Some will criticize you. Some will support you. We are an opinionated lot. Know this... I do this for a living. My house and office is a mix of technology because we do a lot of testing. Our wireless network consists of UAP-AC-Pro, UAP-AC-M, U6-Mesh, U6-Enterprise, U6-Enterprise-IW. Notice, no U7-Pro. Why waste the energy on this power hungry beast that is noisy (fan to cool it), runs hot as hell, and makes my power meter run fast. I get ZERO benefit from it. We are doing a major upgrade here too... all new switches, fiber, replacing all the older UAPs with U6-Mesh or U6-Plus or Pro. Depends on what I have laying around. Needs are simple... reliable, stable WiFi. I am not looking to win the Indy 500 of speed tests. I want to finish the race in one piece and not wreck along the way.

The new U6 and U7 equipment can coexist with older APs. You can theoretically banish the old APs into obscure corners where you just need basic SU-MIMO WiFi.

The big concern with U7 (and U6-Enterprise) is the fact that new 6 GHz APs need 2.5 GbE PoE+ connections to reach their full potential. Without upgrading your wired Ethernet infrastructure, they are just expensive wall decorations.

You are otherwise extremely vague about the rest of your network, speed requirements, and whether this is home or other setting. Without more information, it is hard to provide any more advice.
