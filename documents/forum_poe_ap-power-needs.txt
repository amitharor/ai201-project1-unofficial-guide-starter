Read the data sheet for each model and add up their power requirements. They all take around 5-8W. You should be fine.

 

The in-wall can draw more than that if you plug in something that needs POE and enable POE passthrough.

I'd strongly recommend against Netgear (or any of the consumer brands) -- they put a lot of pretty pictures on the box, and a lot of bullet points in the datasheets; but when it comes down to it, they tend to fall short of the mark.

Netgear stuff isn't bad, it's just not UniFi.  There's nothing particularly bad about consumer gear - I actually have an old dumb TrendNet 8 port (4 PoE) switch on the back of my desk running a UVP-Executive and a GrandStream phone, and they have been working fine for years.
