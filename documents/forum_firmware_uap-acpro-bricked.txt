3 years ago
SOLVED: Turns out I had a rogue Ubiquiti UniFi Network application running on a PC from 3 years ago (prior to UDM PROs) at IP address dot 170 that was picking up the AP and causing it to be 'offline or not adopted from the perspective of the UDM Pro SE console application running at 192.168.1.1! As soon as I uninstalled that software, the AP reboots and comes back up on the right network just fine!

Unbelievable how impossibly hard it was to uninstall the Network application on the Windows 10 PC-Which I am positive I tried to do years ago as well! Had to kill the 'java' process first before it could be uninstalled by windows. (You get a `UniFi is still running and cannot be uninstalled` error message if it is still running).

Also shame on Ubiquiti's support community forum software for not allowing me to post whenever there is an Address in the posting--because I kept having to OMIT it and block it out just to post, I never noticed the bad inform address! This really must be fixed! It really was crucial information to solving this!
