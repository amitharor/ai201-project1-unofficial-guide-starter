UDM-xxx is an all in one console (router, firewall, application server for Network, Access, Protect, Talk, etc).

The UDM is a small all-in-one with limited function, more for residential or SOHO. Nothing more.

UDR is a simple all in one targetting home users or SOHO. Like the UDMs but not rack mounted and not very robust. More a paper weight that can be dressed up to look like R2D2 or a Minion. Hopefully that gives you my impression of it!

UXG-xxx is a router/firewall/security gateway. These require UniFi Network running on a UCK-G2-Plus or your own controller somewhere (self hosted). Nothing runs on them (no Protect, Access, etc).

The UCG-Ultra is a cheap (aimed at developing markets) gateway. It is the UDM basically, but scaled back a smidge. All in one but very limited. The UXG-Max is described above as UXG-XXX.

You can create a new post to discuss and that is fine @mwcormier but the Aggregation switch has 8 10GbE SFP+ ports on it. You can plug in the UDM-PM into one of them and provide network out to 7 devices potentially (if you do not enable LAG on some ports). You can also plug the UNVR-Pro into the same switch that has your cameras attached for the SFP+ if you want as the traffic will not need to traverse your network for cameras to UNVR-Pro and can stay on the same switch. So if you take that approach you can then have 2 switches on your Aggregation switch plus the UDM-PM connected which will leave you 5 ports free for more switches to be connected into the network.

The one thing I would suggest you use, if possible, is OM3 or OM4 Fiber versus DAC. DAC is a great alternative if you can keep it within the distance but I would use RJ45 copper as last usage as those modules limits how many you can have in the Aggregation Switch. The other reason I suggest OM4 is that when you decide to upgrade the switches all you have to change out is the module as the cables are capable of 100GbE within the distance of the spec for those speeds. Swap out switch and module not cable is one of the easier upgrades.
